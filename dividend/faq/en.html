1. What are cryptocurrency dividends?

Dividends are a portion of profits or rewards that a project distributes to its token holders.

2. How to receive dividends?

To participate in the distribution, you need to prove ownership of a governance token and send it to a special contract during each epoch.

3. What is an "epoch"?

This is a fixed time period during which the blockchain accounts for token holders. Only those who sent tokens to the contract within this epoch will receive rewards.

4. Where should tokens be to participate?

They must be manually sent to the project's contract each time. Simply holding them in a wallet or on an exchange is not enough.

5. How to prove participation in an epoch?

You need to send tokens to the specified contract before the epoch ends. The blockchain will automatically record the transaction.

6. When are dividends paid?

Usually after each epoch ends, but exact timing depends on the project.

7. Is anything required besides sending tokens?

Sometimes it's necessary to:

- Confirm participation for each new epoch

- Keep tokens locked until the epoch ends

8. How to check the dividend amount?

It depends on:

- The number of tokens sent

- The total reward pool

9. Where to track information?

- Project's official website

- Smart contract

- Blockchain explorers

Important: Participation must be confirmed every epoch! Simply holding tokens does not grant reward rights.
